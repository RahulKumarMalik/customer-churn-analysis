05 — Key Analytical Findings
📊
Pareto Revenue Concentration
The top 20% of customers generate 37% of revenue — and the top 50% account for over 71%. This validates classic Pareto distribution in subscription businesses and argues for segment-specific retention investment.
TOP 20% → 37% REVENUE · TOP 50% → 71% REVENUE
⏱️
45-Day Inactivity is the Churn Trigger
Churned customers show a consistent pattern of 45+ days of purchase inactivity before their churn date. This creates a measurable early-warning window for outreach — customers hitting 30 days inactive should immediately enter a retention campaign.
45+ DAY INACTIVITY → PRIMARY CHURN SIGNAL
💳
Plan Type is the Strongest Predictor
Basic plan customers churn at 39.1% vs Enterprise at 8.1% — a 4.8× difference. Price sensitivity and perceived value gap are the dominant drivers. Downgrade offers and feature unlock campaigns are more effective than discounts alone.
BASIC 39.1% vs ENTERPRISE 8.1% → 4.8× DIFFERENCE
🚨
"At Risk" Segment — Highest Revenue Exposure
The At Risk RFM segment has a 59.9% churn rate — the highest of any segment — while still holding ₹938 Lakh in revenue. This makes it the single highest-priority retention target: high revenue at imminent risk.
₹938L AT RISK · 59.9% CHURN RATE
📣
Paid Search Has the Worst Retention
Paid Search customers churn at 30.1% — significantly above Organic (23.6%) and Social Media (23.2%). Acquisition channel quality directly predicts long-term retention, suggesting CAC reallocation away from paid search toward referral and organic.
PAID SEARCH 30.1% vs ORGANIC 23.6% CHURN
💰
12% Churn Reduction = ₹15L Annual Recovery
A targeted retention campaign reaching the Critical + High risk cohorts, achieving a 12% win-back rate, projects to ₹15,00,000+ in annualised MRR recovery at an estimated 8-10× campaign ROI — based on avg monthly spend of churned customers.
12% WIN-BACK → ₹15L ARR RECOVERY · 8-10× ROI
